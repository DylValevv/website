Roles: Tech Artist, Programmer, Designer
Tools Used: Unity, Axis Neuron, Maya

Gone Phishing is a low-poly isometric fishing game that mixes the concept of phishing, a fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information, with the literal physical act of fishing. Players compete in teams of two to construct Bait and phish from a pool of potential victims milling about in the cafe below. 

Gone Phishing was made in under 36 hours and won Best Game at Hack@WPI 2020 and was showcased in Made in MA 2020

Reflection

Gone Phishing was a game that tested my capabilities in working diligently and with a team under a tight time constraint. Developed in under 36 hours for Worcester Polytechnic Institute's annual Hackathon, I had to learn how to quickly develop quality assets for the team. After designing the gameplay loop with the team, I threw on a motion capture suit and began recording quality animation data for the player and fish-people animations. It is here where I learned how to transfer data from Axis Neuron into Maya, and utilized the built in Quick Rig and HIK system to animate models made by our artists to expedite the art pipeline. Additionally, I was tasked with helping program and debug back-end and front-end systems, along with coding the fishing hook functionality. Here the team ran into difficulties with source control and merging code infrastructure handled by different members, however we amended this by collectively debugging each member's script at a time, collaborating fully to ensure that everything operated smoothly and that the game could be finished on time.
