Reflection

Trials of Midnight was made during MassDigi's Summer Innovation Program by a team of 6 developers located all across the Northeast. As both lead programmer and producer, I was able to refine my skills as a team leader and developer, while expanding my knowledge with the experience of working with an entirely new team of varying personalities and skills. Working with similarly sized teams in the past, this project provided the opportunity for me to apply what I've learned from previous mistakes, ensuring that team morale was good and that quality work was being completed in a timely manner. This was done using Miro with an Agile workflow, with weekly sprint goals and daily standups to ensure communication was clear between the Art and Programming subteams, which I attribute the success of much of our achieved goals to.
My greatest challenge presented itself in doubling as producer and lead programmer. This was at first a stressful balance to strike, as I had to keep track of many important aspects of the project, while focusing on meeting my own deadlines. However, as the team established its rhythm, I learned to lessen the mental load by entrusting the managment of art tasks to our Lead Artist, giving me more bandwidth to develop the game's core systems. These systems were built with a modular backend architecture using a singleton design pattern and scriptable objects with C#. In doing so, our Lead Designer was able to easily balance the game with a streamlined custom inspector within Unity. Furthermore, I wanted to expand my skills as a technical artist, so I sought to create tools to expedite the art pipeline. I did this using Unity's shader graph in order to reduce the need of cutting up sprite sheets and using a single material for every entity's animation. This allowed for animation implementation to be incredibly quick and easy, requiring only an import of the sprite sheet and creating a material to animate characters. In doing so, we were able to reduce project size, optimize, and easily scale the animations with varying UI.
Over the course of development, we showcased the game to many industry professionals with playtesting and iterated on their constructive feedback. This further enhanced our skills as iterative developers, learning to prioritize which feedback was feasible and which was out of scope, helping us maintain our game's essence and core vision.
At the end of the program, we successfully built for iOS and Android on our planned launch dates and prepared built-in analytics to further enhance the game.​
