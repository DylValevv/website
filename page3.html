Reflection

Sliptime Sleuth is a game developed by Sunburst Studio, a group of passionate developers where I proudly work as Lead Programmer and Designer. Sliptime Sleuth was the team's first project, serving as a space where many things were learned. At the start, we were unsure about how to conduct development, so we decided to work exclusively in the realms of our roles, where I designed levels and their puzzles. However, we quickly learned this was a big mistake, as members not involved in design became confused and frustrated with the ever-changing design of the game. To fix this, we decided that development and design should work simultaneously and side-by-side. To do so, I coordinated and lead weekly meetings to ensure that team dynamics were positive and that communication and collaboration were clear, along with maintaining asset lists and documentation for level design. From there on out, design iterations were quicker, pipelines became more effective, and team spirit and passion was high. After exhibiting the game at many events in the Greater Boston Area and learning new skills, we soon realized that the game's build was difficult to iterate on after playtesting. This lead to the decision to rebuild the game from the ground up so we could deliver a quality product we were proud of in time of release. During this process, I was responsible with re-building the game's back-end architecture, coding modular systems that allowed for quick level implementation that scaled easily with the number of levels we had planned and frequent iterations in level design, along with programming and implementing the UI systems.
