Reflection

Paper Protector was my first personal game development project that I completed from start to finish. Because of this, I gained the majority of my game development skills during its 2 month development period. In the beginning, I sought out to learn C# and the Unity engine, which led me to learn how to properly use prefabs, scriptable objects, coroutines, and optimization. More importantly, I learned how to properly tackle problems in a project, both technically and mentally. When debugging, I realized I must look at the project as a whole, and learned that as a project becomes more complex, the more mistakes will be shown and the more out of scope the design becomes without proper foundations and planning. Because of this, I re-built the game's code from the ground up with better back-end systems and modular code, and I re-scoped the gameplay and design. From there on out, I continued working closely with my Lead Artist and on the game until it was ready for launch, whereupon I learned how to use Adobe Premiere Pro and created a trailer for launch on the Google Play store. Currently, I continue updating the game with bug fixes and optimization tweaks.
